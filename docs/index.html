Modeling of Missing Data

Modeling of Missing Data

Jerry Chia-Rui Chang

May 2018

Abstract

The Senior Executive Service (SES) hold key leadership positions across agencies within the Federal government. These leaders serve as the primary link between top political appointees and the rest of the Federal workforce. The selection of career-reserved SES, often considered as lack of diversity, possesses great challenges to an effective SES program. Therefore, this study aims to understand factors that impact the SES promotion outcome, where data about federal employees’ personal and career information would be analyzed.

Due to recording error and time constraints, multiple variables have missing data, with some over 80% missing. To address potential data analysis challenges caused by missing data, we adopt Multiple Imputation techniques to impute missing data. First, multiple completed datasets are generated through Multivariate Imputation by Chained Equations (MICE), where Classification and Regression Tree (CART) is specified as the conditional distribution. Posterior Predictive Check (PPC) is then applied to check the robustness of the model. Second, we fit a semi-parametric spline logistic regression model on one completed dataset. The model coefficients are estimated by two approaches - Frequentist’s maximum likelihood and Bayesian’s posterior distribution. Lastly, we combine each completed dataset’s analysis, where inference is conducted on the combined model results.
