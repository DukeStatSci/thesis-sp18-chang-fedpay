Modeling Federal employees data to predict promotions

Modeling Federal employees data to predict promotions

Chia-Rui (Jerry) Chang

May 2018

Abstract

The Senior Executive Service (SES), similiar to a private company’s Chief Executive Officer, is a key leadership position within the Federal government. Federal employees with the SES position serve as the primary link between top political appointees and the rest of the Federal workforce. The selection of career-reserved SES, often considered as lack of diversity by gender, race, and ethnicity, possesses great challenges to an effective SES program. Therefore, this study aims to understand factors that impact the SES promotion outcome, where data about federal employees’ personal and career information would be analyzed.

Due to recording error and time constraints, multiple variables have missing data, with some over 80% missing. To address potential data analysis challenges, we adopted multiple imputation technique to impute missing data. First, we generated 12 completed datasets through multivariate imputation by chained equations, with Classification and Regression Tree specified as the conditional distribution. Predictive check was then used to assess the robustness of imputation results. Second, we experimented various statistical models based on one completed dataset. Piecewise linear regression model yielded appropriate fit to the data and was applied to all completed datasets. Lastly, we conducted inference through combining model results of each completed dataset.
